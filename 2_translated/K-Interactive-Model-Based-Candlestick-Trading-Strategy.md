### Overview

This strategy determines buy and sell signals based on the形态和互动模式 of candlesticks. It mainly utilizes breakouts through support and resistance levels, while also incorporating specific candlestick patterns to assist in decision making.

### Strategy Logic

The strategy primarily identifies the following candlestick patterns:

1. Small Bullish Candle: Close is higher than open with a short real body.
2. Inverted Hammer: Open is near the high, close is near the low.
3. Doji Star: The previous candle crosses the current doji candle.

In conjunction with pattern recognition, support and resistance levels are set. The specific logic is:

1. When a Small Bullish Candle appears above the resistance level, a buy signal is generated.
2. When an Inverted Hammer appears below the support level, a sell signal is triggered.

This combination filtering helps to filter out false signals and makes trading decisions more reliable.

### Advantage Analysis

The advantages of this strategy are:

1. Combines chart patterns and numerical indicators for more robust signals.
2. Support/resistance levels prevent unnecessary whipsaws.
3. Candlestick pattern recognition is relatively simple, easy to understand and implement.
4. Customizable parameters suit different market environments.

Overall, the strategy is relatively simple and practical for testing ideas and assisting manual trading.

### Risk Analysis

This strategy also has some risks:

1. Candlestick pattern recognition can be unreliable, leading to incorrect signals.
2. Incorrectly set support/resistance levels can negatively impact performance.
3. Unable to handle black swan events and significant volatility.
4. Insufficient backtest data may lead to overestimating the effectiveness of the strategy.

Mitigations mainly involve strict parameter validation, tuning of support/resistance levels, and incorporating stop losses to control risk. Additionally, extensive historical data backtesting is necessary to properly evaluate the actual performance of the strategy.

### Optimization Directions

This strategy can be improved in several areas:

1. Incorporate more types of candlestick pattern detections for additional trade signals.
2. Optimize support/resistance calculation methods to better align with market trends.
3. Add secondary indicators such as moving average distance and trading volume changes to aid decision making.

> Source (PineScript)

``` pinescript
/*backtest
start: 2023-12-13 00:00:00
end: 2023-12-20 00:00:00
period: 1m
basePeriod: 1m
exchanges: [{"eid":"Futures_Binance","currency":"BTC_USDT"}]
*/

//@version=5
strategy("Candlestick Pattern Strategy", overlay=true)

// Input for support and resistance levels
supportLevel = input(100, title="Support Level")
resistanceLevel = input(200, title="Resistance Level")

// Detecting Candlestick Patterns
isDoji = close == open
isPressure = close < open and open - close > close - open
isInvertedHammer = close > open and low == (close < open ? close : open) and close - open < 0.1 * (high - low)
isHammer = close > open and close - open > 0.6 * (high - low)

// Buy and Sell Conditions
buyCondition = isHammer and close > resistanceLevel
sellCondition = isInvertedHammer and close < supportLevel

// Strategy Logic
strategy.entry("Buy", strategy.long, when = buyCondition)
strategy.close("Buy", when = sellCondition)

// Plot Buy and Sell signals on the chart
plotshape(series=buyCondition, title="Buy Signal", color=color.green, style=shape.triangleup, location=location.belowbar)
plotshape(series=sellCondition, title="Sell Signal", color=color.red, style=shape.triangledown, location=location.abovebar)

// Plot Support and Resistance levels
plot(supportLevel, color=color.green, title="Support Level")
plot(resistanceLevel, color=color.red, title="Resistance Level")
```
