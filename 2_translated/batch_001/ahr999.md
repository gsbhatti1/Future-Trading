#### Overview
This strategy is based on the AHR999 and AHR999X indicators. By comparing the current price with the harmonic mean price and logarithmic price, it determines whether Bitcoin is overbought or oversold, and accordingly conducts dollar-cost averaging investments and bottom-fishing operations. Additionally, the strategy includes a take-profit condition to partially sell holdings when the AHR999X indicator falls below a certain threshold.

#### Strategy Principle
1. Calculate the harmonic mean of Bitcoin's historical prices as a reference for the long-term equilibrium price.
2. Based on the number of days since Bitcoin's inception, calculate the corresponding logarithmic price as a reference for the long-term trend price.
3. Calculate the current AHR999 indicator, which is the product of the ratio of the current price to the harmonic mean price and the logarithmic price.
4. Calculate the current AHR999X indicator, which is three times the product of the ratio of the harmonic mean price to the current price and the ratio of the logarithmic price to the current price.
5. Based on the value of the AHR999 indicator, decide whether to conduct dollar-cost averaging investments or bottom-fishing. Dollar-cost averaging is performed when AHR999 is within a set range, and bottom-fishing is conducted when it falls below the lower limit.
6. Based on the value of the AHR999X indicator, determine whether to take profits. When AHR999X falls below a set threshold, sell a certain percentage of the holdings.

#### Advantage Analysis
1. The strategy is based on Bitcoin's long-term equilibrium price and trend price, providing a solid theoretical foundation and interpretability.
2. The introduction of the harmonic mean reduces the impact of short-term fluctuations, making judgments more robust.
3. By combining the AHR999 and AHR999X indicators, the strategy can effectively capture Bitcoin's overbought and oversold conditions and optimal profit-taking opportunities.
4. Combining dollar-cost averaging and bottom-fishing allows for gradual position building at relatively undervalued prices, resulting in lower average costs.
5. The inclusion of take-profit operations enables capturing gains during relatively overvalued periods while retaining most holdings for long-term appreciation.

#### Risk Analysis
1. The strategy relies on the AHR999 and AHR999X indicators. If Bitcoin's price behavior significantly changes in the future, rendering these indicators ineffective, the strategy may face substantial risks.
2. The harmonic mean is sensitive to the length of historical price data. If the selected historical price period is unreasonable, it may negatively impact strategy performance.
3. The calculation of logarithmic price is based on the number of days since Bitcoin's inception. If Bitcoin's long-term growth trend changes, the logarithmic price may lose its reference value.
4. The strategy may encounter the risk of insufficient funds for bottom-fishing when prices are relatively high, potentially missing timely entry opportunities.
5. Profit-taking operations may cause the strategy to miss additional gains if prices continue to rise.

#### Optimization Directions
1. Optimize the thresholds for AHR999 and AHR999X indicators to identify the most suitable ranges for dollar-cost averaging, bottom-fishing, and profit-taking.
2. Introduce additional indicators such as trading volume and volatility to assist in determining trends and overbought/oversold conditions.
3. Optimize the calculation method of the harmonic mean, such as appropriately adjusting the weights of historical prices to reduce dependence on distant historical data.
4. Dynamically adjust the amounts for dollar-cost averaging and bottom-fishing, increasing investment when prices are lower and reducing investment when prices are higher.
5. Optimize profit-taking operations, such as dynamically adjusting based on price trends and holding costs, to balance returns and risks.

#### Summary
The AHR999 Mean Reversion Strategy determines Bitcoin's overbought and oversold conditions by comparing the current price with historical equilibrium and trend prices, and executes dollar-cost averaging, bottom-fishing, and profit-taking operations accordingly. This strategy has a solid theoretical foundation and interpretability. By combining two indicators, it can effectively capture Bitcoin's price fluctuation patterns. function harmonicMean(x) {
	if (x.length === 0) {
		return undefined;
	}
	var reciprocalSum = 0;
	for (var i = 0; i < x.length; i++) {
		if (x[i] <= 0) {
			return undefined;
		}
		reciprocalSum += 1 / x[i];
	}
	return x.length / reciprocalSum;
}
