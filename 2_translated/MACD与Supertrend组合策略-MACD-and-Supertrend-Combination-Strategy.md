#### Overview
This article introduces a trading strategy that combines two technical indicators: MACD and Supertrend. The strategy uses the crossover signals of the MACD indicator and the trend direction of the Supertrend indicator to determine entry and exit points, aiming to profit from trending markets. The main idea of the strategy is to go long when the MACD crosses above the signal line and the Supertrend is green, and to go short when the MACD crosses below the signal line and the Supertrend is red. The crossover of the MACD signal line and the MACD line serves as the exit signal.

#### Strategy Principle
The strategy uses the MACD indicator and the Supertrend indicator to generate trading signals. The MACD is calculated by subtracting the slow moving average (default 26-period) from the fast moving average (default 12-period), and then calculating the 9-period moving average of the MACD as the signal line. A bullish crossover occurs when the MACD line crosses above the signal line, indicating a long signal; while a bearish crossover occurs when the MACD line crosses below the signal line, indicating a short signal. The Supertrend indicator combines the ATR volatility indicator. When the price is above the Supertrend line and the Supertrend line is green, it indicates an uptrend; conversely, if the price is below the Supertrend line and the Supertrend line is red, it indicates a downtrend. The strategy goes long when the MACD forms a bullish crossover and the Supertrend is green, and goes short when the MACD forms a bearish crossover and the Supertrend is red, following the trend. At the same time, when the MACD signal line crosses the MACD line, the position is closed to control drawdown.

#### Strategy Advantages
1. Combining trend and momentum indicators, it can better capture trending markets. The MACD indicator is forward-looking and can judge trend reversals in advance; while the Supertrend indicator can effectively filter out range-bound markets. Together, they can establish positions in a timely manner during trending markets, thereby increasing strategy returns.
2. Using signal line crossover as the exit signal allows for timely stop-loss and take-profit actions. The MACD signal line reflects short-term price trends. When it crosses the MACD line, it signals that the trend may be reversing; hence, closing positions promptly can effectively control drawdowns and avoid significant losses.
3. The strategy logic is clear, with simple rules that are easy to implement and optimize. This strategy relies on only two common technical indicators, making its calculation methods mature and facilitating backtesting and optimization based on different parameters.

#### Strategy Risks
1. Risk of parameter applicability for MACD and Supertrend selection. Both the MACD and Supertrend indicator calculations involve time period parameters. Optimal parameters may vary across different markets and assets; fixed parameters could render the strategy ineffective in certain market conditions.
2. The risk of lagged identification of trend reversals. As a trend-following indicator, the MACD may experience a delay relative to price movements, leading to false signals during the initial stages of trend reversal. Similarly, Supertrend also exhibits some latency in identifying trend reversals.
3. The risk of frequent trading in range-bound markets. This strategy may generate frequent bullish and bearish crossover signals in range-bound markets, resulting in excessive trading that incurs higher transaction costs and lowers overall returns.

#### Strategy Optimization Directions
1. Optimize MACD and Supertrend parameters for different underlying assets and timeframes using methods such as exhaustive search or genetic algorithms to find the optimal parameter combinations, improving strategy adaptability and stability.
2. Add additional filtering conditions based on MACD bullish and bearish crossovers, including changes in trading volume and price breakouts, to further confirm trend reversals and minimize false signals.
3. Introduce position management and stop-loss/take-profit mechanisms such as ATR-based stop-loss and percentage-based stop-loss/take-profit to control per-trade risk and enhance the strategy's drawdown control and profit-to-loss ratio.
