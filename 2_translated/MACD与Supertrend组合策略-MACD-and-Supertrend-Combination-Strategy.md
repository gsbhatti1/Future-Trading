#### Overview
This article introduces a trading strategy that combines two technical indicators: MACD and Supertrend. The strategy uses the crossover signals of the MACD indicator and the trend direction of the Supertrend indicator to determine entry and exit points, aiming to profit from trending markets. The main idea of the strategy is to go long when the MACD crosses above the signal line and the Supertrend is green, and to go short when the MACD crosses below the signal line and the Supertrend is red. The crossover of the MACD signal line and the MACD line serves as the exit signal.

#### Strategy Principle
The strategy uses the MACD indicator and the Supertrend indicator to generate trading signals. The MACD is calculated by subtracting the slow moving average (default 26-period) from the fast moving average (default 12-period), and then calculating the 9-period moving average of the MACD as the signal line. A bullish crossover occurs when the MACD line crosses above the signal line, indicating a long signal; while a bearish crossover occurs when the MACD line crosses below the signal line, indicating a short signal. The Supertrend indicator combines the Average True Range (ATR) volatility indicator. When the price is above the Supertrend line and the Supertrend line is green, it indicates an uptrend; when the price is below the Supertrend line and the Supertrend line is red, it indicates a downtrend. The strategy goes long when the MACD forms a bullish crossover and the Supertrend is green, and goes short when the MACD forms a bearish crossover and the Supertrend is red, following the trend. At the same time, when the MACD signal line crosses the MACD line, the position is closed to control drawdown.

#### Strategy Advantages
1. Combining trend and momentum indicators, it can better capture trending markets. The MACD indicator is forward-looking and can judge trend reversals in advance; while the Supertrend indicator can effectively filter out range-bound markets. The combination of the two can establish positions in a timely manner in trending markets, improving strategy returns.
2. Using signal line crossover as the exit signal, it can stop loss and take profit in a timely manner. The MACD signal line reflects the short-term price trend. When it crosses the MACD line, it means that the trend may reverse; therefore, closing positions in a timely manner can effectively control drawdowns and avoid larger losses.
3. The strategy logic is clear, and the rules are simple, making it easy to implement and optimize. The strategy uses only two common technical indicators, with mature calculation methods, allowing for convenient backtesting and optimization based on different parameters.

#### Strategy Risks
1. Risk of parameter applicability in MACD and Supertrend selection. The calculations of both MACD and Supertrend indicators involve time period parameters, and the optimal parameters may vary across different markets and underlying assets. Fixed parameters might cause the strategy to fail under certain market conditions.
2. Risk of lag in identifying trend reversals. As a trend-following indicator, the MACD signals may have some lag relative to price movements; hence, false signals might still be generated during the initial stages of trend reversal. The Supertrend also has some delay in judging trend reversals.
3. Frequent trading risk in range-bound markets. In range-bound markets, the strategy may generate frequent bullish and bearish crossover signals, leading to excessive trading activity and higher transaction costs, which could reduce overall strategy returns.

#### Strategy Optimization Directions
1. Optimize MACD and Supertrend parameters for different underlying assets and timeframes. Methods such as exhaustive search or genetic algorithms can be used to find the optimal parameter combinations to improve the adaptability and stability of the strategy.
2. Add additional filtering conditions on top of the MACD bullish and bearish crossover signals, such as changes in trading volume and price breakouts, to further confirm trend reversals and reduce false signals.
3. Introduce position management and stop-loss/take-profit mechanisms, such as ATR-based stop-loss or percentage-based stop-loss and take-profit, to control single trade risks and improve the drawdown control and risk-reward ratio of the strategy.
4. Consider adding cycle filtering or commodity rotation rules to reduce trading frequency in range-bound markets and enhance the overall return-risk ratio of the strategy.

#### Conclusion
This article introduces a trading strategy based on MACD and Supertrend indicators, which leverages the trend judgment of MACD and the directional filtering of Supertrend to trade in trending markets while using signal line crossovers to close positions promptly to control drawdowns. The main advantage of this strategy is its simple logic and strong ability to capture trends, but it also faces risks such as parameter applicability, signal lag, and frequent trading in range-bound markets. Future improvements could involve optimizing parameters, adding filtering conditions, introducing position management mechanisms, and considering cycle and commodity selection rules to achieve more stable returns.
