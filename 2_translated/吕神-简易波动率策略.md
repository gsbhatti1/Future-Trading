A Simple Volatility Strategy

Those familiar with me know that I don’t particularly enjoy alpha games. Instead, I lean towards beta research. Why? Well… to be honest, I can’t really explain it. Feel free to imagine what makes sense for you.

If you’re interested, you can leave a comment or send a private message to the author of this account. If your logic is clear and unique, the author will send you a small red packet.

Quantitative strategy development has its pros and cons. For beginners, it’s incredibly challenging both on the "technique" side—coding—and the "logic" side—strategy thinking. Both are equally important; they shouldn’t be neglected. The strategy I’m introducing today was inspired by an old report from Huatai Securities years ago. However, this strategy has evolved significantly beyond what was originally mentioned in that report.

The algorithm uses a rolling return volatility based on the logarithm of price changes over a certain period. According to this volatility range, it calculates the highest and lowest values within a fixed period and sets these as the upper and lower channels, respectively. When the price breaks through the upper channel, an entry is made. The average of the upper and lower channels serves as the exit line.
